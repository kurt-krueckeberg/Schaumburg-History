A sketch by Dr. Hubert Höing, archive director (of the Historical Working Group for the Study of Schaumburg)

Today's Schaumburg district is one of the smaller districts in Lower Saxony with an area of 675 square kilometers and a population of 166,000. It is located at the foot of the German low mountain range around the city triangle Rinteln - Stadthagen - Bückeburg, conveniently located on the Weser, on the Mittelland Canal, on the federal highways B 65 and B 83, on the A 2 motorway and on the Cologne - Hanover - Berlin railway line.

It is naturally blessed with fertile soil and a mild climate. Despite all the changes in the regional and administrative reforms from 1973 to 1977, Schaumburg remained a separate cultural area, the history of which is still alive today; a district that - despite some breaks - is characterized by the continuity of its historical area.
