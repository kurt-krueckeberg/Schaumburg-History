Runification in the County of Schaumburg 1977


Wappen des Landkreises Schaumburg: in Rot mit blauem
Schildrand ein silbernes Nesselblatt (genehmigt 1979)

The end of Schaumburg-Lippe came with the administrative reform of the 1970s: The administrative district of Schaumburg-Lippe (based in Stadthagen) and the administrative district of Grafschaft Schaumburg (based in Rinteln) were merged by legislative decree on August 1, 1977. The new administrative district of Schaumburg was created, with its administrative seat in Stadthagen.

The borders of the new county were rounded off: In the north, the municipality of Wiedensahl was added from the district of Nienburg, in the southeast, among others, the municipality of Lauenau from the dissolved district of Springe. The town of Hessisch Oldendorf was given to the district of Hameln-Pyrmont, and Steinhude, including the Steinhuder Meer, was given to the town of Wunstorf—and thus to the district of Hannover. Apart from these losses, the new county of Schaumburg has since more or less regained the size of the old county of Schaumburg that it had at the time of its division in 1647. After more than 330 years of separation, "reunification" took place, which was initially opposed, sometimes fiercely, but is now generally accepted after 25 years of existence. Despite administrative reform and population fluctuation after World War II, a sense of community has remained alive among most Schaumburgers to this day as in probably few other areas of Germany. It is rooted, among other things, in the awareness of shared history.
